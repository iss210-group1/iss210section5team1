About

Disability is a condition that makes it difficult for a person to undertake certain activities or tasks that are common for most individuals. This could be the result of developmental, mental, or physical disabilities. In order to fully engage in society, people with disabilities may need assistance or accommodations, and they may experience stigma or prejudice as a result of their condition. It's critical to keep in mind that people with disabilities are an inherent aspect of the human experience and should be treated with respect. Governments all around the world are developing programs and policies to enhance the quality of life for people with disabilities in an effort to overcome this disparity. In order to guarantee that individuals with disabilities are treated with respect and given access to equal opportunities, organizations like the ADA and UNCRPD have created legislation. There are a lot of well-known persons who have succeeded despite having a disability. One of the most well-known scientists and public people who had a neurological condition was Stephen Hawking. People like him demonstrate that a disability need not be a barrier to one's aspirations.

Video Title

Hannah Barham-Brown is a doctor diagnosed with an extremely rare disease called Ehlers-Danlos Syndrome, a group of inherited disorders that affect connective tissues — primarily skin, joints and blood vessel walls. People view her as either a celebrity or as someone who doesn't belong in the medical field, both are exhausting for her as she can do anything any other doctor can do. She goes on to say that 1 in 5 people in the UK are disabled, yet the employment gap is 30% and that 30% is wasted talent, which is her main point. It doesn't really matter if someone is mentally or physically disabled because everyone has their own abilities. Sure, not everyone is qualified to do certain jobs; however, that shouldn't stop them for working somewhere else. Barham-Brown hopes to be a role model and leader for more equality in the workplace and change the stereotypes that the disabed are unfir for work.

Title of Image 6

Disability Network Capital is a group located in Michigan that serves the greater Clinton, Eaton, Ingham, and Shiawassee counties. Using their combined experience and peer-support they're able to serve those with disabilities alongside their families. They partner with schools, business, and community agencies to further improve the quality of life of those in need. When exploring their webpage one can find various resources and events that the group holds, not only to help those with disabilities but business and people around the area. Their mission statement goes as follow "Transforming thinking to improve the environment and quality of life for people with disabilities."
